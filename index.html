The current US Navy carrier-borne aircraft include the F/A-18E/F Super Hornet, a versatile multirole fighter used for air superiority and ground attack missions, along with its electronic warfare variant, the EA-18G Growler. The E-2D Advanced Hawkeye serves as an airborne early warning and control aircraft, providing enhanced radar coverage and situational awareness. For cargo and personnel transport to and from aircraft carriers, the C-2A Greyhound is utilized. Additionally, the MH-60R Seahawk plays a vital role in anti-submarine warfare, anti-surface warfare, and search and rescue missions. The MQ-25 Stingray, an unmanned aerial vehicle, is designed for aerial refueling of manned aircraft, primarily the F/A-18 Super Hornet. These aircraft represent a diverse and capable fleet that supports the US Navy's carrier strike groups in various missions and operations.
