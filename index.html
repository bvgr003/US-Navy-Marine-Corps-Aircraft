The current US Navy carrier-borne aircraft include the F/A-18E/F Super Hornet, a versatile multirole fighter used for air superiority and ground attack missions, along with its electronic warfare variant, the EA-18G Growler. The E-2D Advanced Hawkeye serves as an airborne early warning and control aircraft, providing enhanced radar coverage and situational awareness. For cargo and personnel transport to and from aircraft carriers, the C-2A Greyhound is utilized. Additionally, the MH-60R Seahawk plays a vital role in anti-submarine warfare, anti-surface warfare, and search and rescue missions. The MQ-25 Stingray, an unmanned aerial vehicle, is designed for aerial refueling of manned aircraft, primarily the F/A-18E/F/G Super Hornet. These aircraft represent a diverse and capable fleet that supports the US Navy's carrier strike groups in various missions and operations.

Carrier Borne Aircraft



The US Marine Corps operates several types of amphibious aircraft for various missions. Alongside the MV-22 Osprey, the Corps also uses the CH-53E Super Stallion for heavy-lift transport and the UH-1Y Venom for utility and transport. Additionally, the Corps utilizes the AH-1Z Viper for attack helicopter missions. These aircraft, in conjunction with other Marine Corps assets, provide a comprehensive amphibious and aerial capability to support Marine Corps operations worldwide.
